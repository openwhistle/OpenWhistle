    Compliance

    Telephone Reporting Channel

    HinSchG §16 Abs. 1 Nr. 2 requires that internal reporting channels include a telephone hotline option in addition to the written channel provided by OpenWhistle. This page guides you through setting up a compliant telephone reporting channel.

    Legal requirement: Organisations with 50 or more employees and all public authorities are legally required to offer at least two reporting channels — one of which must allow verbal (telephone) reporting pursuant to HinSchG §16 Abs. 1 Nr. 2. OpenWhistle fulfils the written channel; you must provide the telephone channel separately.

    Compliance Checklist

    Verify that your telephone reporting channel meets the following requirements:

    • ☐ A dedicated telephone number is published internally and accessible to all employees.
    • ☐ The channel is confidential — calls are handled by trained, impartial staff or an external ombudsman.
    • ☐ The person answering is bound by a confidentiality obligation (§9 HinSchG).
    • ☐ A verbal report can be transcribed and submitted to OpenWhistle under a case number by staff — with the whistleblower's consent (§17 Abs. 1 HinSchG).
    • ☐ The whistleblower is informed about the channel via internal communications, intranet, and/or poster.
    • ☐ Call recordings, if any, are compliant with DSGVO and §9 HinSchG confidentiality provisions.
    • ☐ The channel is operational during normal business hours (or 24/7 for critical industries).
    • ☐ If using an external ombudsman, their engagement letter includes a DSGVO Art. 28 Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

    Implementation Options

    Internal Hotline

    Designate a trained employee (Compliance Officer, HR) as the channel operator. Assign a dedicated direct-dial number. Document the duty schedule.

    Best for: organisations with an existing compliance or legal function.

    External Ombudsman

    Engage a certified external ombudsman (Ombudsmann) or compliance service provider. They receive calls on your behalf and forward anonymised reports to OpenWhistle.

    Best for: smaller organisations or where impartiality is critical.

    Integration with OpenWhistle

    1. Staff enters the verbal report via OpenWhistle's submission form. Ask the caller's consent before creating the report on their behalf.
    2. Select "Confidential" mode if the caller consents to having their name recorded, so that the whistleblower receives a dedicated case number.
    3. Provide the case number and PIN to the caller so they can follow up on their report via the whistleblower status page.
    4. Document the telephone report in the admin notes (never in the public message thread visible to the whistleblower).

    Recording Prohibition Notice (§10 HinSchG)

    Important: §10 HinSchG prohibits the covert recording of verbal reports without the whistleblower's explicit consent. Any recording must be disclosed at the start of the call and the whistleblower must actively agree. Covert recordings may lead to criminal liability under §201 StGB (violation of spoken word confidentiality).

    Recommended call opening script (adapt to your organisation):

    "Thank you for calling the confidential reporting channel of [Organisation Name]. This call is handled by [Name/Role], who is bound by strict confidentiality. Your report will be treated in accordance with the Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz (HinSchG). This call is not being recorded. You may remain anonymous. Please go ahead with your report."
