    How It Works

    1. Every day at 03:00 UTC the retention scheduler runs.
    2. Reports with status "closed" whose closed_at timestamp is older than {{ retention_days }} days are permanently deleted.
    3. All associated data is removed: messages, attachments, notes, audit-log references (case number preserved in the auto-deletion audit entry).
    4. An immutable audit log entry (report.auto_deleted) is written for each deleted report, recording the case number, closed date, retention period, and compliance basis.

    Note: Reports in any status other than "closed" are never auto-deleted, regardless of their age.

    Legal Basis

    GDPR Art. 5(1)(e) — Storage Limitation

    Personal data must not be kept longer than necessary for the purpose for which it was collected. Retaining closed reports indefinitely would violate this principle.

    HinSchG §12 Abs. 3 — Documentation Obligation

    Reports and their follow-up must be documented for at least 3 years after the procedure is concluded. The default retention period of 1095 days (3 years) meets this minimum. Do not set RETENTION_DAYS below 1095 unless you have specific legal advice to do so.

    GDPR Art. 17 — Right to Erasure

    Individuals may request deletion of their personal data. The auto-deletion mechanism and the existing manual 4-eyes deletion workflow both support fulfilment of erasure requests.
