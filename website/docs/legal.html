HinSchG — Key Paragraphs Reference

This page summarises the paragraphs of the Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz (HinSchG) most relevant to deploying and operating OpenWhistle. For legally binding text, always consult the official source.

Official Sources →
Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz (HinSchG) — Bundesgesetz gesetze-im-internet.de/hinschg/
→
EU Directive 2019/1937 — EUR-Lex eur-lex.europa.eu — CELEX:32019L1937
In force

The HinSchG entered into force on 2 July 2023. This document covers the paragraphs most relevant to organisations deploying a digital whistleblower channel. It does not constitute legal advice.

§8 Confidentiality — Vertraulichkeit

The identity of the whistleblower must be kept confidential at all times. Information that directly or indirectly allows identification of the whistleblower may only be disclosed:

  • With the explicit consent of the whistleblower, or
  • When required by law (e.g., criminal prosecution requires disclosure)
Implementation in OpenWhistle

No IP addresses, no browser fingerprints, and no metadata that could identify the whistleblower are stored anywhere in the system. The four-layer IP anonymisation architecture ensures that even the system operator cannot identify the whistleblower without their cooperation.

See Security Architecture → IP Anonymisation for the technical implementation.

§12 Obligation to Establish Internal Reporting Channels

Companies with 50 or more employees and all public authorities must establish internal reporting channels. The reporting channel must:

  • Allow reports in writing, verbally, or both
  • Guarantee the confidentiality of the whistleblower's identity
  • Be accessible to employees of the organisation
Implementation in OpenWhistle

OpenWhistle provides the technical reporting channel. The written-form digital submission (the /submit route) satisfies the written reporting requirement. The organisation deploying OpenWhistle is responsible for meeting the corresponding organisational requirements (designating a case handler, etc.).

§16 Internal Reporting Office — Interne Meldestelle
§16 Abs. 1 — Operation

The internal reporting office must be operated by an internal employee designated for this purpose, or by a third party (e.g., an external provider). Multiple organisations may share a single reporting channel.

§16 Abs. 3 — Secure Channel

Reports must be receivable via a secure channel that ensures confidentiality. The whistleblower must be identifiable by the reporting office (via their case reference) to enable follow-up, but not identifiable to anyone else.

§16 Abs. 7 — Oral Reports

If the whistleblower requests an oral report, the reporting office must provide the opportunity (telephone, video conference). A record must be made and provided to the whistleblower for approval.

Implementation in OpenWhistle

The case number + UUID4 PIN system satisfies the "identifiable by reporting office" requirement while maintaining full anonymity to all other parties. The written-form digital submission satisfies the written reporting requirement.

Oral report handling (§16 Abs. 7) requires a separate organisational process outside OpenWhistle — the admin portal provides a text-based follow-up thread, but not voice/video capability. Organisations with oral reporting obligations should supplement with a telephone process and use OpenWhistle to document the record.

§17 Follow-Up by the Internal Reporting Office — Rückmeldungen

This is the most critical paragraph for bidirectional communication and SLA compliance.

§17 Abs. 1 — Acknowledgement of Receipt (7 days)

The reporting office must acknowledge receipt within 7 days of receiving the report.

Exception: The 7-day deadline may be waived only if the whistleblower explicitly requests no acknowledgement AND sending an acknowledgement would compromise the whistleblower's anonymity.

Implementation
  • System tracks submitted_at and acknowledged_at timestamps
  • Admin dashboard shows a warning when the 7-day SLA is approaching or exceeded
  • Acknowledgement can be automatic (on submission) or manual (admin action)
§17 Abs. 2 — Feedback within 3 Months

The reporting office must provide feedback within 3 months of the acknowledgement. The feedback must include:

  • What measures have been taken or are planned
  • The reasons for those measures (or why no measures will be taken)
Implementation
  • System tracks feedback_due_at = acknowledged_at + 90 days
  • Admin dashboard shows a "3-month SLA" warning when approaching
  • Admin can send a feedback message to the whistleblower through the secure thread
§17 Abs. 3 — Communication Channel

The follow-up communication must be done via the same secure channel through which the report was submitted. The whistleblower must be able to receive messages back through the OpenWhistle interface.

Implementation
  • Bidirectional message thread (case messages) is required by law under this paragraph
  • The whistleblower reads admin replies using their PIN + case reference at /status
  • The whistleblower can also send follow-up messages to the admin
§26 Data Protection — Datenschutz
§26 Abs. 1 — Legal Basis

The processing of personal data in connection with internal reporting channels is lawful under DSGVO Art. 6(1)(c) (legal obligation) and Art. 6(1)(e) (public interest task), as far as required for compliance with HinSchG.

§26 Abs. 2 — Confidentiality of Third Parties

If a report contains information about third parties (e.g., the person being reported), their identity must also be treated confidentially until the report has been investigated to a sufficient degree.

§26 Abs. 3 — Data Retention (3 years)

Personal data must be deleted after 3 years following the completion of a case, unless longer retention is required for ongoing proceedings or the whistleblower consents to longer retention.

Implementation
  • Reports have a closed_at timestamp set when status changes to "closed"
  • The admin interface provides a hard deletion function for cases past the 3-year mark
  • Hard deletion removes the report, all associated messages, and the PIN hash
  • A future version will include automated retention enforcement via a scheduled task

DSGVO Requirements for Whistleblower Systems

The following table maps DSGVO articles to their implementation within OpenWhistle:

Requirement Article Implementation
Data minimisation Art. 5(1)(c) No IP logging, no unnecessary database fields, no analytics
Privacy by design Art. 25 Self-hosted, anonymised from the first request, no external CDN calls
Lawful basis Art. 6(1)(c) HinSchG §26 provides the legal basis for processing whistleblower report data
Right to erasure Art. 17 Reports are hard-deletable. Hard deletion removes all associated records.
Security of processing Art. 32 TLS in transit, bcrypt password hashing, Redis session encryption at rest
Data breach notification Art. 33 72-hour notification to supervisory authority is an organisational obligation — OpenWhistle provides structured audit logs to support this
No international transfer Art. 44ff Fully self-hosted; no data ever leaves the operator's infrastructure
